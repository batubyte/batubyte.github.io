The Reality of Smoking

Smoking is a very important problem worldwide, being one of the leading causes of preventable diseases. Smoking may start as a habit but its effects on your body are long lasting and extremely dangerous.

  • Your lungs will be harmed, reducing your breathing capacity and often leading to a chronic obstructive pulmonary diease. (COPD)
  • The risk of many cancers including lung, throat, mouth and bladder gets increased.
  • Raises the chance of heart disease, stroke, and blood vessel damage.
  • Weakens your immune system, making it harder to fight against infections and stay healthy.
  • Contributes to your mental health issues like anxiety and depression by affecting your brains chemistry.

Some Statistics: Who Is Affected?

Understanding the scale of smoking helps highlight why quitting is critical:

Statistic Value / Fact
Worldwide Smokers Over 1.1 billion people in the world smoke.
Smoking Deaths Approximately 8 million deaths annually.
Youth Smoking About 10% of teenagers worldwide smoke regularly.
Secondhand Smoke Exposure More than 1.2 million non-smokers die annually from secondhand smoke.

Benefits of Quitting

Quitting smoking improves your health in every way:

  • Within 20 minutes: Your heart rate and blood pressure begin to drop.
  • After 12 hours: Carbon monoxide levels in your blood normalize.
  • After 2-12 weeks: Circulation improves and lung function increases.
  • After 1 year: Risk of heart disease is half that of a smoker.
  • After 5 years: Stroke risk is similar to a non-smoker.
  • After 10 years: Lung cancer death rate is about half that of a smoker.

Effective Methods of Quitting

Quitting smoking can be difficult, but the following strategies increase your chances of success, helping you quit with ease:

  • Set a quit date and ask your friends and family for support.
  • Use nicotine replacement therapies (patches, gum, lozenges).
  • Try prescription medications approved by doctors.
  • Join support groups or counseling sessions.
  • Track progress with quit-smoking apps like QuitNow.
  • Avoid triggers such as stressful situations or certain social groups.
  • Adopt healthier habits like exercising, hobbies or relaxation techniques.

For help and more information, call the free quit line: ALO 171 or visit World Health Organization Tobacco Facts.

Social and Environmental Impact

Smoking doesn’t just affect the smoker, it impacts people around them and our planet as well:

  • Secondhand Smoke: Non-smokers exposed to smoke in the air have increased risks of respiratory infections, asthma, and heart disease.
  • Litter: Cigarette butts are the most common form of litter worldwide and can take years to decompose.
  • Deforestation: Tobacco farming contributes to deforestation and soil degradation.
  • Air Pollution: Cigarette smoke releases harmful toxins into the air, affecting air quality.

Myths and Facts About Smoking

Myth Fact
“Light cigarettes are safer.” Light or low-tar cigarettes are just as harmful as regular ones.
“Smoking helps reduce stress.” Nicotine creates addiction and anxiety, it doesn't reduce stress; quitting reduces stress in the long term.
“It’s too late to quit after years of smoking.” It’s never too late, quitting smoking always improves your health and life expectancy.

A Lesson from The Little Prince

In The Little Prince by Antoine de Saint-Exupéry, there is a character called "The Drunkard" who drinks to forget his mistakes and escape the bad reality of his life. However, drinking only increases his feelings of shame, trapping him in a painful, self-destructive cycle.

This story helps us understand addiction: people often turn to harmful habits to avoid difficult emotions, but these habits usually make things worse. It shows why quitting is so hard, because the addiction is not just physical, but also emotional and psychological.

Like the Drunkard, smokers can feel stuck in this cycle. But by recognizing how these patterns work, and with the right support and healthier habits, breaking free is possible. It starts with awareness and kindness toward ourselves.

True Change Comes From Within

No matter how much we try to help you with your addiction, you have to want it yourself. Our support can only reach you if you truly want to change with your own desire.

No one can force you to quit unless you are ready to take action yourself. Support, guidance, and resources can light the path, but it’s your own decision and strength that will move you forward, towards health and your well being. You are the one who must take the first step and when you do, everything will begin to change rapidly.

Healthy Alternatives and Positive Habits

Replacing harmful habits with positive actions builds strength and happiness:

  • Engage in sports or regular physical activities.
  • Explore creative arts like painting, music, or writing journals.
  • Practice mindfulness, meditation, and deep breathing.
  • Join clubs or social groups that promote healthy lifestyles.
  • Set personal goals and celebrate achievements.

Your school counselor is available for extra help and advice.
